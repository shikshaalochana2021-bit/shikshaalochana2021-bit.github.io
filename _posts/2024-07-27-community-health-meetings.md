A distinctive feature of Shiksha Alochana's health programme is the role of student leadership. Each participating school has a Child Cabinet, and the health minister of that cabinet plays an active role in organising and leading community meetings on health.

In a single year, **288 community meetings** were organised across the 150 participating schools. These meetings brought together:

- Student cabinet health ministers
- Class teachers and school staff
- Parents and guardians
- Community members

At each meeting, student health ministers presented the objectives of the School and Community Health Programme, building shared understanding and community investment in the health of their school. These meetings have become an important forum for parents to raise health concerns and for schools to respond collectively.
